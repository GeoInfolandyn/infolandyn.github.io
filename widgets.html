How does Dynamicland work?

The main objective of the INFOLANDYN project is to design and implement an Information System that allows the development and provision of products related to the dynamics of the Earth's surface derived from time series of remote sensing, at different scales. To achieve this goal, a process of design and development of a web-based Information System will be carried out, which includes the selection and configuration of remote sensing tools, as well as the development of algorithms for the generation of products, among other relevant elements.

Arquitecture of the Information System Dynamicland-Qubidy


The products that will be developed include phenological and dynamic indicators, as well as cartographic products. To identify the most relevant and useful indicators and products, a process of research and development will be carried out, which will allow the implementation of suitable algorithms and techniques to generate these products. In addition, the continuous review and updating of the developed products will be guaranteed, thanks to a data and product management plan that will be implemented within the framework of the project.

Products and levels

Cartographic products at two levels will be developed: (1) first level will consist of products representing the general attributes of vegetation index time series based on statistical and phenological methods, such as inflection points or autocorrelation values at specific lags, (2) in a second level the “Ready to Use” products will consist on environmental information related to the processes happening at the land surface, such as maps of the beginning of the growing cycles or the date of harvest. In addition, analysis and reports will be generated.

LEVEL A

Raw data and algorithms:
Level A of the INFOLANDYN project involves the collection, processing, and analysis of raw remote sensing data, as well as the development of algorithms to transform the data into useful information. The algorithms will be designed to generate phenological and dynamic indicators, as well as cartographic products. It is important to ensure that the algorithms are efficient, accurate, and adaptable to different types of data and products. Level A represents the foundation of the project.

LEVEL B

Time series analysis:
Time series analysis involves statistical and machine learning methods to extract meaningful information from time-dependent data. In the context of land surface dynamics, time series analysis is used to identify patterns and changes in vegetation index time series derived from remote sensing data. This analysis helps to understand the dynamics of vegetation growth, the timing and duration of growing seasons, and the impacts of environmental factors such as climate variability and land use change.

LEVEL C

Indicators:
Indicators are measurements or variables that provide insight into complex systems or processes, and in the context of land surface dynamics, they help assess global change impacts and inform management decisions related to natural resources and agricultural lands. This project aims to develop clear and objective indicators of land surface dynamics derived from remote sensing time series, including vegetation intra-annual dynamics, inter-annual patterns, trends, structural changes, forecasting models, onset and end of growing season, and climate impact indicators.

LEVEL D

Environmental statistics:
Environmental statistics is a subfield of statistics that involves the collection, analysis, and interpretation of environmental data to identify patterns and trends, assess data accuracy and reliability, and make predictions about future environmental conditions. It plays a crucial role in understanding complex environmental systems and informing management decisions related to natural resources and the environment.

LEVEL E

Paper & Reports:
Reports are a key output of the land surface dynamics information system developed in this project, providing a comprehensive summary of the analyses and findings related to land surface dynamics and their impacts on global change. They include information on the methods used to develop indicators and cartographic products, results of statistical and machine learning analyses, ecological meaning of indicators, and recommendations for management and policy decisions based on scientific evidence. Reports aim to support sustainable natural resource management and land use practices.

User Management

"User Management: Addressing the Needs of Different Roles" INFOLANDYN, an information system designed for monitoring global change impacts through land cover dynamics modeling, caters to diverse user needs through effective user management. It provides valuable indicators derived from remote sensing time series, including phenological and dynamics indicators, forecasting models, and climate impact indicators. The system offers clear and objective products at various scales, supporting sustainable natural resource management and policy-making. User management in INFOLANDYN ensures optimized user experiences by implementing role-based access control, assigning specific permissions and privileges based on user roles.

"Addressing the Needs of Different Roles"

This approach promotes data security while enabling users to access relevant resources. Additionally, comprehensive user support, including onboarding, training, and timely assistance, fosters effective communication, collaboration, and productivity. By addressing the distinct requirements of different roles, INFOLANDYN empowers users to make informed decisions and drive positive environmental impact.
